The History of Hornets

The History of Hornets and Bees The history of hornets and bees is deeply intertwined, with both being part of the Hymenoptera order. Hornets belong to the family Vespidae, while bees are in the family Apidae. Despite their close relationship, they have diverged significantly at the family level, leading to distinct biological groups. Bees are known for their social structures and complex interactions with flowering plants, while hornets are known for their aggressive behavior and predation on bees. The relationship between hornets and bees is a predator-prey relationship, with hornets targeting bees for protein to feed their larvae. This interaction is a significant aspect of their history and the dynamics of their populations.
-Museum of Earth
