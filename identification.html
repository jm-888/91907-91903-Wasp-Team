Close-up Features

Yellow-legged hornets (Vespa velutina) are one of the largest invasive hornet species. Key features include:

  • Bright yellow legs contrasting with dark brown body.
  • Yellow face with strong mandibles used for hunting other insects.
  • Elongated thorax with dark abdomen and yellow-orange stripes.
  • Fuzzy thorax near the head, unlike smooth wasps.

Differences from Other Species

Confusing hornets with native bees or wasps is common. This is a Honeybee. Differences include:

  • Size: up to 3 cm long, bigger than most native wasps.
  • Leg Color: bright yellow vs brown or black in natives.
  • Flight: fast, hovering near hives and plants.
  • Body: more elongated thorax, dark brown abdomen with yellow stripes.
  • Behavior: predatory, hunting bees and insects for food.

Hives & Nests

Yellow-legged hornet nests are usually spherical and made from chewed wood pulp. They are often found:

  • High up in trees, sometimes over 10 meters above ground.
  • Under roof eaves or inside shrubs and dense vegetation.
  • Containing hundreds to thousands of hornets at peak season.

Hives can be active from spring to autumn. Never try to remove a nest yourself; hornets will aggressively defend it.

Habits & Behavior

Yellow-legged hornets are predatory and territorial. Important points:

  • Active during daylight, resting at night.
  • Prey mainly on honeybees, but also other insects and occasionally fruit.
  • Queen lays eggs in spring; colony grows rapidly through summer.
  • Workers forage up to 1 km from the nest.

Understanding their behavior helps in avoiding conflicts and safely reporting sightings.

Safety Tips

To stay safe around yellow-legged hornets:

  • Do not disturb nests yourself, contact local authorities.
  • Keep children and pets away from hornet activity.
  • Wear protective clothing if working near trees or shrubs where nests may be present.
  • Report sightings to official monitoring teams like the Wasp Team.
  • Educate yourself and others on identification to prevent accidental contact.
