About Us

This website was created to educate people in New Zealand about hornets, the risks they can pose, and how to stay safe. While hornets are not as common as wasps in New Zealand, they can still be dangerous if disturbed, especially for people who are allergic to stings. Many people confuse hornets with wasps or bees. This site helps explain the differences, how to identify them, and what to do if you find a nest.

Identify Wasps

OUR GOALS

  • Raise awareness about hornet safety in New Zealand
  • Help people identify hornets and tell them apart from bees and wasps
  • Provide clear, easy-to-understand information for students and families
  • Explain what to do if someone is stung
  • Encourage reporting of unusual or invasive species
  • Support the protection of pollinators like the European honey bee
  • Reduce misinformation and unnecessary panic about hornets
  • Promote safe and responsible behaviour around wildlife

Background

Why We Built This

📢
A real issue, not enough awareness

Yellow-legged hornets were first detected in New Zealand in 2023. Despite being a serious biosecurity threat, many people still don't know what they look like or what to do if they spot one.

🐝
Native bees are at risk

These hornets prey heavily on honeybees and native pollinators. Without action, they could cause significant harm to New Zealand's ecosystems and food production.

🏫
A school project with a purpose

This site was built as part of a school web development project. We chose this topic because we wanted our work to be useful — something that could genuinely inform and help people in our community.
