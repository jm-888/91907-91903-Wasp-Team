Identification

Yellow-legged hornets are larger than bees with distinctive yellow legs and an orange-brown body. Adult workers are 17-20mm long with a characteristic pattern.

Key features: Black head, brown thorax, dark abdomen, and bright yellow legs. Learn to spot them quickly and accurately.

Location & Habitat

Commonly found throughout northern New Zealand during summer and autumn months. They build large paper nests in trees, roofs, and sheltered locations.

These invasive species thrive in warm climates and are most active from November through April. Early detection is critical for control efforts.

Why They're Harmful

Yellow-legged hornets prey on native bees, severely threatening New Zealand's biodiversity and pollinator populations. They can sting multiple times.

Their impact extends to human health and safety. Stings can cause serious allergic reactions, and their aggressive nature makes them particularly dangerous around populated areas.

Safety & Reporting

If unsure about a sighting, don't worry—no harm in reporting! Submit your observation through our form and help protect New Zealand's ecosystems.

Follow safety guidelines when observing potential hornets. Keep a safe distance, never attempt removal yourself, and contact professionals immediately.
